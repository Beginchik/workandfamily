Eliza Carthy is an English folk musician known both for singing and playing the violin. She is the daughter of singer/guitarist Martin Carthy and singer Norma Waterson, who are also English folk musicians. In addition to her solo work, she has played and sung with several groups, including as lead vocalist with Blue Murder. She has been nominated twice for the Mercury Music Prize for UK album of the year, has won eight BBC Folk Awards, and has also been given an MBE for services to folk music. When she released an album of collaborations with her mother, entitled Gift, a BBC reviewer wrote: 'The gift in question here…is a handling of talent from generation to generation.'
